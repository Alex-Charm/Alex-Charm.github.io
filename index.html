What is a Website?

A website is a collection of publicly accessible, interlinked web pages that share a single domain name. Websites can be created and maintained by an individual, group, business, or organization to serve a variety of purposes.

Together, all publicly accessible websites constitute the World Wide Web (WWW).

Types of Websites & Examples

Websites come in many shapes and sizes depending on their goals. Here are the most common types:

Website Type Purpose Examples
Search Engines To help users find information on the internet. Google.com, Bing.com
E-Commerce To buy and sell goods and services online. Amazon.com, eBay.com
Social Media To connect, communicate, and share content with others. Facebook.com, X.com, Instagram.com
Informational / Media To provide news, educational content, or references. Wikipedia.org, BBC.com
Streaming / Entertainment To consume video, music, or gaming content. YouTube.com, Netflix.com, Spotify.com

What Do We Use Websites For?

Websites have completely transformed how society functions. We primarily use them for:

  • Information Sharing: Finding answers to questions, reading the news, and learning new skills.
  • Communication: Staying in touch with friends, family, and colleagues globally.
  • Business & Commerce: Allowing companies to market products, handle customer service, and process sales 24/7.
  • Entertainment: Watching movies, listening to music, playing games, and reading blogs.
  • Education: Accessing online courses (e-learning), research papers, and digital libraries.

5 Basic Steps to Create a Website

Building a website is easier than ever. Here is the standard roadmap to getting your site live on the internet:

  1. Plan Your Website: Define your website's purpose (e.g., a personal portfolio) and map out what pages you will need.
  2. Register a Domain Name: Your domain name is your website's address on the internet (like www.yourname.com).
  3. Choose a Web Hosting Provider: A web host provides the server space where your website's files live so people can access it.
  4. Build the Site: You can code the site yourself using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, or use a CMS like WordPress or Wix.
  5. Publish and Maintain: Upload your files to your host to take it live, and keep your content updated!
